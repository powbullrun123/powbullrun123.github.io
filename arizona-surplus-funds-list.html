What Are Arizona Surplus Funds?

When a property is foreclosed and sold at auction, the final sale price sometimes exceeds the amount owed on the mortgage or liens. When this happens, the remaining money becomes foreclosure surplus funds (also known as excess proceeds or foreclosure overages).

These funds are typically held by the court until the rightful party files a claim. In many cases the person entitled to the funds is the former homeowner or their heirs.

Unfortunately, many Arizona residents never realize they are entitled to this money. As a result, thousands of dollars in surplus funds remain unclaimed across the state each year.

Why Surplus Funds Go Unclaimed

Many foreclosure surplus funds remain unclaimed because:

  • Former homeowners move after foreclosure
  • Mail notifications are never received
  • Heirs are unaware the funds exist
  • The court claim process appears complicated

Because of these issues, foreclosure surplus funds can remain unclaimed for years.
