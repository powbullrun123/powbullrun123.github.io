What Are Foreclosure Surplus Funds?

When a foreclosed property sells at auction for more than the debt owed, the remaining funds are held by the county or trustee. These funds legally belong to the former homeowner or other eligible lienholders.

This money is often referred to as:

  • Foreclosure surplus funds
  • Excess proceeds
  • Trustee sale overages
  • Unclaimed foreclosure funds

Who Can Claim Surplus Funds?

The following parties may have the right to claim excess proceeds:

  • Former property owners
  • Junior lien holders
  • Heirs of the former homeowner
  • Other legally entitled parties
