What Are Arizona Surplus Funds?

Under A.R.S. § 33-812, when a property is sold at a trustee sale and the winning bid exceeds the total debt, the remaining balance becomes trustee sale excess proceeds — commonly referred to as surplus funds.

These funds are deposited with the county treasurer and may be claimed by the former property owner or eligible lienholders in statutory priority order.

Maricopa County Surplus Funds

Maricopa County is the largest foreclosure market in Arizona. Each year, significant amounts of foreclosure excess proceeds are deposited with the Maricopa County Treasurer.

Filing a Maricopa County surplus funds claim requires:

  • Proof of identity
  • Proof of ownership at time of sale
  • Recorded trustee’s deed documentation
  • Proper statutory claim submission

Improper filings may delay or invalidate a claim.

Other Arizona Counties Holding Excess Proceeds

Surplus funds are regularly held in:

  • Pima County
  • Pinal County
  • Yavapai County
  • Mohave County
  • Coconino County

Each county processes trustee sale overages under Arizona statute, but administrative procedures may vary.

How to Claim Surplus Funds in Arizona

  1. Identify whether excess proceeds exist from the trustee sale.
  2. Verify statutory eligibility under A.R.S. § 33-812.
  3. Prepare required documentation.
  4. Submit formal claim to the county treasurer.
  5. Await review and distribution approval.

Deadlines apply. Failure to file properly can result in permanent forfeiture of funds.
