    About Us

    What Is Surplus Fund Recovery?

    When a property is sold at a foreclosure auction or tax sale for more than what was owed, the remaining balance — called surplus funds or excess proceeds — is held by the county court. Most former owners never know this money exists.

    At Rightful Returns Recovery, we specialize in foreclosure overage recovery and tax sale surplus fund claims. We track down these funds, file the legal claims, and reunite former homeowners with money that is rightfully theirs.

    There is no cost to you upfront. We work on a contingency basis — meaning we only earn a fee if we successfully recover your unclaimed surplus funds.

    What We Recover

    Types of Surplus Funds We Recover

    Foreclosure Surplus Funds

    When a lender forecloses and the auction price exceeds the mortgage balance and fees, the excess is owed to the former homeowner. These foreclosure overages are held by the county court and must be claimed within a limited window.

    Tax Sale Excess Proceeds

    When a county sells a property for unpaid taxes and the sale generates more than the tax debt, the tax sale surplus belongs to the former owner. We search tax sale records across Arizona, Georgia, Texas and all 50 states.

    HOA & Lien Sale Overages

    Properties sold by HOAs or lienholders can also generate excess proceeds owed to the prior owner. These are often overlooked but can represent significant unclaimed funds sitting in county accounts.

    Got Questions?

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are surplus funds after a foreclosure? +
    Surplus funds, also called foreclosure overages or excess proceeds, are money left over after a foreclosure or tax sale pays off all debts. The former property owner is legally entitled to claim these funds — but most people never know they exist.
    How do I claim foreclosure surplus funds? +
    You must file a legal claim with the county court or agency holding the funds. The process involves identifying the surplus, gathering documentation, and submitting a petition. Rightful Returns Recovery handles this entire process for you at no upfront cost. Call us at 1 (800) 428-2022 or (480) 274-3499.
    How much does surplus fund recovery cost? +
    There is absolutely no upfront cost. We work on a contingency basis — we only earn a fee if we successfully recover money for you. If we find nothing, you pay nothing. Zero risk.
    How long do I have to claim surplus funds? +
    Claim windows vary by state. In Texas you have 2 years, Georgia up to 5 years, and Arizona varies by sale type. Acting quickly is always recommended — deadlines are strictly enforced and funds can be permanently lost.
    Which states do you serve? +
    We actively serve Arizona, Georgia, and Texas, and can assist homeowners in all 50 states through our network of licensed attorneys. Contact us regardless of your state — call 1 (800) 428-2022.
    What is the difference between surplus funds and unclaimed property? +
    Surplus funds refer to excess proceeds from foreclosure or tax sales held by county courts. Unclaimed property is a broader category held by the state comptroller. Rightful Returns Recovery specializes in surplus fund and foreclosure overage recovery.
