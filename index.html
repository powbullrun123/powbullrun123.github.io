What Are Surplus Funds?

When a property sells for more than what is owed during a foreclosure or tax sale, the remaining balance becomes legally classified as surplus funds or excess proceeds.

These funds belong to the former property owner or qualified lienholders — not the lender and not the county. Filing deadlines apply and claims must meet legal requirements.

State-Specific Surplus Funds Laws

Arizona Surplus Funds – A.R.S. § 33-812

Arizona trustee sale excess proceeds are deposited with the county treasurer. Maricopa County and other Arizona counties regularly hold foreclosure surplus funds.

Texas Tax Sale Surplus – Tax Code § 34.03

Texas district clerks hold excess proceeds following tax foreclosure sales. Filing windows are often limited to two years.

Georgia Tax Sale Overages – O.C.G.A. § 48-4-5

Georgia counties maintain tax sale overage accounts in all 159 counties. Proper statutory documentation is required to claim funds.
