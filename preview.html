How Surplus Funds Work in Georgia

When a property is sold at a tax sale or foreclosure auction in Georgia, the proceeds are first applied to:

  • Mortgage balance or tax debt
  • Interest and penalties
  • Attorney fees and court costs

If the sale generates more than the total debt owed, the remaining amount becomes excess funds. Under Georgia law, those funds belong to the former property owner or other legally entitled parties.

However, these funds are not automatically mailed to you. A formal claim must be filed with the county holding the money.

Georgia Claim Deadlines

The Georgia Surplus Funds Claim Process

Filing a claim in Georgia typically involves:

  1. Identifying the surplus in county records
  2. Verifying ownership and legal entitlement
  3. Gathering required documentation
  4. Submitting a formal written claim or petition
  5. Resolving competing claims if multiple parties are involved

Errors or missing documents can delay approval or result in denial. That’s where professional assistance can make the difference.
